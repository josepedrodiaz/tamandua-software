Hello. We're Tamandua — two engineers in Buenos Aires, building WordPress since 2010. This page is the whole pitch: what we do, how we work, and where to write if you want to work together.

What we build

Three things, mostly.

Custom themes, built from scratch to fit the brand, and written so the next developer can open the code and get straight to work.

Plugins that do one job well. When your workflow needs something WordPress doesn't do out of the box, we build that piece, document it, and wire it into what you already run.

And when the project calls for it, whole platforms — architecture, build, launch, support — with performance, SEO and accessibility built in from the first commit.

How we work

We listen before we quote. The first call is about your business and what's actually getting in the way. Then you get a proposal in plain language: what we'll build, how long it takes, what it costs. If a smaller project would serve you better, we'll tell you — talking someone into more than they need is how you end up working with them only once.

While we build, you can watch. Work lands on a staging site every few days, so you're never a month in the dark wondering what's going on. And you talk directly with the two people writing the code. No account manager forwarding your emails, no "delivery lead" who's never opened the repo, no layer of managers between you and the people building it — and no flights on your invoice for what could've been a Google Meet.

And that overhead gets paid twice: once by you, and once by whoever's building your site — in pressure, cut corners, and a fried head shipping at 2am. You pay more and get worse. Two of us, no middle layer, so nobody pays that bill. Just the work.

When we launch, you get documentation, training for your team, and a clean handover path. If you ever decide to take the work in-house, it goes with you. That's the deal.

The AI part

We should be upfront about this, because it's a real part of how we work: we use AI all day, and we've gotten good at it.

Every commit gets reviewed by the two of us plus an AI reviewer in the pipeline, roughly like having a third engineer on call. It flags things earlier and catches regressions faster. The code is still ours. We just ship it about three times faster than we used to, and fewer bugs make it to production.

If AI tooling makes you uneasy, we're probably not your studio. We've spent the last few years learning to use it well without losing the plot, and we'd rather tell you that up front.

What it costs

We're in Argentina, so senior work here costs less than it would from a studio in Berlin or Austin — same quality, cheaper cost of living. We quote a fixed price per project after the first conversation, and it doesn't move.
